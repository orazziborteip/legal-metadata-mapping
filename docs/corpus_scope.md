# Corpus scope

## Research boundary

The corpus was assembled to examine how legal conditions relevant to data access, sharing, reuse, governance, and AI-training data can be represented, evidenced, or bounded through metadata in Common European Data Space settings.

The selection is purposive rather than exhaustive. Inclusion records a source's function in the analysis; it does not assert that every retained provision applies to a particular organisation, system, model, dataset, or operation.

## Temporal boundary

- Substantive legal baseline: **1 April 2026**.
- Source checks: **17 July 2026**.
- Publication dataset: **19 July 2026**.

Adoption, publication, entry into force, and application are treated as distinct legal events.

## Principal instruments

The corpus contains normative statements from five principal instruments:

| Instrument | Function in the analysis | Registered units | Normative statements |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Regulation (EU) 2025/327 — European Health Data Space | Dataset descriptions and data-quality labels | 83 | 21 |
| Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 — Data Act | Data-space interoperability and standardisation conditions | 58 | 25 |
| Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — AI Act | Data-governance requirements for selected high-risk AI systems | 101 | 22 |
| Regulation (EU) 2022/868 — Data Governance Act | Protected public-sector data reuse and related governance conditions | 82 | 66 |
| Directive (EU) 2019/790 — DSM Copyright Directive | Text-and-data-mining rights, reservations, access, and use boundaries | 34 | 11 |

## Supporting sources

Ten supporting units are drawn from Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and Regulation (EU) 2018/1725 where their provisions are needed to interpret a retained proposition. They do not add normative statements to the selected principal population.

The resulting public corpus contains 368 provision and supporting units and 145 normative statements.

## Inclusion criteria

A provision or supporting unit is retained where it materially affects:

- an actor or instrument-specific legal role;
- a governed object or data category;
- an access, sharing, reuse, processing, or training operation;
- a purpose, trigger, condition, exception, or temporal rule;
- required information, documentation, evidence, or control;
- a standardisation, interoperability, or conformity route;
- a right, permission, prohibition, power, procedure, or legal consequence; or
- the boundary between machine-readable representation and qualified determination.

Definitions, recitals, related provisions, and external legal rules are retained only where needed to understand a selected normative statement.

## Exclusions and non-claims

The release does not reproduce every provision of every relevant legal instrument. It does not determine:

- the legal classification of a real organisation, system, model, actor, or dataset;
- the lawful basis or legal authority for a concrete processing or training operation;
- ownership, protected status, confidentiality, lawful access, or the effect of a rights reservation for a real dataset;
- satisfaction of open-textured legal standards;
- national implementation beyond the bounded propositions recorded; or
- compliance, enforcement outcomes, or legal effects for a concrete case.

The assessed standards are also bounded. A specification is included because its versioned components were relevant to the comparison performed in the thesis, not because the specification is universally applicable or legally endorsed.

## Extensibility

The 145 normative statements constitute the bounded evidentiary basis for developing and applying the method; they are not a closed catalogue of all EU rules potentially relevant to data access and AI training, especially in an evolving regulatory landscape. The same method can be extended to additional provisions, instruments, standards, and use configurations, provided that each extension repeats the necessary source selection, legal analysis, extraction, and authorial validation. Such extensions may add to or qualify the resulting framework.
