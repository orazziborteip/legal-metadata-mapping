# Methodology

## Purpose

The method translates selected European Union legal provisions into source-linked analytical records and compares their information and evidence needs with existing metadata specifications. Metadata is treated as support for traceability, representation, checking, and review; it is not treated as a substitute for legal or factual judgment.

## 1. Source and corpus selection

Each retained legal source has a stable identifier, official URL, issuer, publication and application information, and a recorded status at the declared research dates. The selection is based on the function of a provision in the research question rather than on an internal priority code.

A provision is retained where it defines or materially affects an actor, legal role, governed object, regulated action, purpose, condition, exception, temporal rule, required information, evidence, procedure, or legal consequence. Definitions, recitals, and related provisions are retained only where necessary to interpret a selected proposition.

## 2. Atomic normative statements

The principal unit of analysis is an atomic normative statement. Each statement remains linked to its source provision and records the legally material elements needed for the bounded analysis, including:

- modality and legal strength;
- addressee and instrument-specific role;
- action and governed object;
- beneficiary or right-holder;
- purpose, triggers, conditions, and exceptions;
- required information, form, control, or evidence;
- supervising or deciding actor;
- consequence or remedy; and
- material cross-instrument dependencies.

The decomposition preserves the enacted proposition and its qualifications. It does not infer a duty, permission, actor, evidentiary threshold, or technical implementation that the source does not supply.

## 3. Applicability

Applicability states are retained as an operational component of the method for assessing concrete use configurations. Applicability is not an intrinsic property of a provision: it depends on the relevant facts, the actors and legal roles involved, the governed object, the purpose, the territorial scope, and the assessment time.

The numerical distribution of states in the analytical corpus is therefore not reported as a substantive result. Without a defined use case, such a distribution would primarily reflect the assumptions adopted and the availability of information rather than the effective applicability of the examined provisions.

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `APPLICABLE` | The established facts satisfy the conditions governing the proposition's application to the recorded configuration. |
| `NOT_APPLICABLE` | At least one necessary condition governing the proposition's application is established to be absent. |
| `UNDETERMINED` | The available facts do not permit a conclusion because a condition governing application has not been established. |
| `CONTESTED` | Materially different unresolved legal readings or factual determinations lead to different applicability results. |
| `SUPERSEDED` | A later assessment replaces the retained historical assessment after a change in law, facts, or configuration version. |

`UNDETERMINED` means that a necessary question remains unanswered. For example, where it has not been established whether an AI system is classified as high-risk under the AI Act, the applicability of an Article 10 duty that depends on that classification remains undetermined. The state describes that defined assessment, not Article 10 or the AI Act in general.

## 4. Standards assessment

Each assessed standard or specification is recorded with its issuer, exact version, publication date, canonical source, formal status declared by the issuer, technical scope, conformance mechanism, available machine-readable artefacts, and principal limitations. The inclusion result is kept separate from formal status: inclusion for the comparison is not endorsement of the specification or evidence of legal sufficiency.

The comparison asks, in ordinary terms, whether an identified information need can be represented by an existing element, requires several elements or additional contextual rules, or is not adequately represented and must remain a matter for qualified assessment. The comparison preserves the meaning and limitations of the selected standard element. Structural conformance cannot establish that recorded information is true, authoritative, adequate, or legally sufficient.

## 5. Authorial review

The author designed the study, selected and interpreted the sources, defined the annotation protocol, and reviewed the published records against the stated criteria and the relevant official legal texts or technical specifications. This process constitutes authorial source and consistency review, not independent legal validation.

## 6. Completion, limitations, and extension

The study is complete within its declared corpus when every retained normative statement is linked to an identifiable source and the relevant analytical stages have been completed. Completion does not imply that the corpus is exhaustive, that a favourable technical representation exists, or that the framework has been validated in a real organisation.

The study recruited no participants and used no real personal or organisational data. Its use configurations and examples are analytical or synthetic. It does not evaluate implementation, usability, organisational effectiveness, production fitness, or legal compliance.

The 145 normative statements constitute the bounded evidentiary basis for developing and applying the method; they are not a closed catalogue of all EU rules potentially relevant to data access and AI training, especially in an evolving regulatory landscape. The same method can be extended to additional provisions, instruments, standards, and use configurations, provided that each extension repeats the necessary source selection, legal analysis, extraction, and authorial validation. Such extensions may add to or qualify the resulting framework.
